Healthy & Fit After 40 Is NOT About the Fitness!

By Julio Mendez • May 30, 2026 • 5 Min Read

Many people believe that it gets harder to be healthy and fit after 40, and in some ways it truly is. I am not going to sugarcoat it, but what truly makes it harder for someone over 40 to get fit is their motivation, will, and consistency.

It may not be your metabolism, genetics, or some hex your ex put on you. Unless you have a serious disease, or your last partner was actually a witch or warlock, I won't mess with the Voodoo that you do.

So Where Do We Start?

I guess the biggest questions are: What is your goal? What are you willing to do for it? What are you willing to give up? How long are you willing to endure the process? This is how you start—it is a change in your mindset, in your thinking and planning. It doesn't begin with a perfect diet, training every day, jogging like a crazy person, or even taking recovery seriously. It begins with you—the real you—not your current physical body or even the things you have done in the past.

Community Accountability

Talk to and join a community of like-minded individuals who share your health and fitness goals. This can provide support, motivation, and accountability. I will be sharing more about how to structure this in future posts.

Where Did I Start? What Was My First Step?

I will start off with what made it hard for me to get fit before I was in my 40s. I have four kids and they were young then, alongside a physically demanding construction job. I also have always gone to school for certificates or degrees. One thing that made it extremely hard was my ex, and she is darn lucky that I am no witch doctor.

So juggling time, exhaustion, brain overload, sadness, or anger—all of this makes it hard. Then I figured out a little secret: if you don't put time into it, then it is simply not important to you.

Now, this is where we go from here. How and when you start is up to you. For reference, I started in 2018 when I was 36, and now I'm 44 in 2026. In 2018, I was 270 lbs (at 5'10") and mostly fat. I went through a divorce, an educational failure, and a job loss. What did I do? Well first, I cried and then hit a gallon of Jack.

But what did I do after that? I got two low-paying full-time jobs and secured a gym membership. I barely paid the bills and bought food with help. But I made it to the gym six days a week, did 45 minutes of cardio, and lifted a bit for 10 minutes. One year later, I was 188 lbs and looking great. Now, that year was hard because there were many other stressors, but I focused on progress.

Hey, why did I go through my story? First, no, it wasn't to have people think my life was hard. It is definitely not to show off. It is to show you I understand we are all busy and we all have hard times. And even in our most difficult times, we can put a little time into getting fit and healthy. Also, if I can do it, I definitely believe and have faith that you, the reader who decided to read this whole blog, can and will do it too.
