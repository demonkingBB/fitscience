Carnivore Diet: The Sizzle, the Science, and the Plaque Risk

By Julio Mendez • June 18, 2026 • 6 Min Read

If you're anything like me, the idea of a diet consisting entirely of juicy ribeyes and crispy bacon sounds less like a chore and more like a dream come true. For many, the first three to six months on a carnivore diet feel like a biological miracle—often called the "honeymoon period."

During this phase, you aren't just eating like a king; your body is undergoing a radical cleanup. By ditching refined carbs, seed oils, and added sugars, you've effectively launched the world's most aggressive elimination diet, removing the primary triggers for gut irritation and systemic inflammation.

The results are often immediate and intoxicating. Because you've cut out the fluff, your insulin levels plummet, which helps reverse insulin resistance and sheds water weight fast—sometimes leading to an average loss of 4kg in just three weeks. Even cooler? Your body enters deep ketosis, producing beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB). This little molecule acts as a high-level signaling agent that effectively "flips the switch" on your immune system's inflammation command center, significantly lowering pro-inflammatory markers like TNF-alpha. You feel lighter, your joints stop aching, and your brain feels like it's finally been plugged into a high-speed charger.

Elimination Warnings

Don't skip the micronutrients! To avoid nutrient gaps in vitamins C, D, and folate, make sure to include organ meats like beef liver in your rotation. Also, watch your butter intake; while delicious, it can spike your LDL cholesterol much faster than the meat itself due to different lipid absorption pathways.

High Cholesterol Dynamics

After the first year or two, the story can get a bit more complicated. While you might still feel like a superhero, your internal "plumbing" might be seeing some different data. The relentless intake of saturated fats can eventually lead to a "clearance problem" in the liver. Essentially, your liver gets so flooded with fats that it downregulates its LDL receptors, meaning those cholesterol particles stay in your bloodstream much longer than they should.

For fit, lean people—a group researchers call Lean Mass Hyper-Responders (LMHR)—this can result in LDL numbers that look like a typo, sometimes soaring between 300 and 500 mg/dL. The dangerous part? This high cholesterol is a silent risk factor; it doesn't hurt, so you might assume you're still "healthy as a horse" because you feel great.

However, the landmark KETO-CTA study found that even in metabolically healthy people with high LDL, there was measurable progression of "soft" plaque in their heart arteries over just one year. Without fiber to protect your gut lining, you also risk metabolic endotoxemia, where bacterial fragments leak into your blood and cause low-grade, chronic inflammation to creep back up.

The Genetic Lottery

So, why do some influencers seem to thrive on nothing but steak for a decade? It likely comes down to the "genetic lottery." Some rare individuals can maintain astronomical cholesterol levels for years without developing a single bit of arterial plaque. But for the vast majority of us, standard human physiology isn't built to handle that kind of lipid overload forever.

The carnivore diet is an incredibly powerful tool for short-term weight loss and managing certain autoimmune conditions, but it's not necessarily a "set it and forget it" lifestyle for everyone. Your genetics, baseline health, and how your liver handles fat are all unique. While the short-term benefits are backed by science, the long-term risks—like nutrient deficiencies in calcium and magnesium or increased cardiovascular risk—make it a path you should tread carefully with regular lab work. Enjoy that steak, but keep an eye on the numbers behind the scenes!
